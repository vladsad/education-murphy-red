We use both/either/neither to talk about two things or people:
    and BOTH and
    or EITHER or 
    not NEITHER not 

Would you like tea or coffee?
    Either. I don't mind (= tea or coffee)
    I don't want either (not I don't want neither)
    Neither (= not tea or coffee)


both + plural:
    both    windows/books/children etc
either  + singular
neither + singular:
    either  window/book/child etc 

both  (of) the..
either  of these/those..
neither of my/your/Paul's.. etc 


both        them
either  of  us
neither     you
